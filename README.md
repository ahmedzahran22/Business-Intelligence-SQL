Sakila Entertainment Business Analysis 🎬📊

Project Overview
This project focuses on transforming raw database records from a movie rental business into actionable business insights. Using Advanced SQL, I analyzed over 16,000 transactions to evaluate revenue performance, customer behavior, and operational efficiency.  

Key Business Insights 💡

Revenue Growth: Achieved a total revenue of $67.4K, with a significant 195% growth surge in July 2005.  

Top Categories: Identified Sports ($4,892) and Sci-Fi ($4,756) as the highest-grossing film categories.  

Customer Segmentation: Classified 599 customers into High, Medium, and Low-value segments based on spending patterns.  

Operational Balance: Discovered a near-perfect 50/50 revenue split between Store 1 and Store 2, indicating consistent regional performance.  

Technical Skills Applied 🛠️
Advanced Joins: Connecting multiple tables (Payment, Rental, Inventory, Film, Category).  

Window Functions: Using RANK() for top-performing films and LAG() for Month-over-Month (MoM) growth analysis.  

Data Aggregation: Summarizing complex metrics using GROUP BY and CASE statements for segmentation.  

Performance Monitoring: Tracking daily revenue pulses and staff transaction efficiency.
